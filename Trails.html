BLUE LAKE TRAIL

[Out and Back]

EASY
MAINTAINED

13.35 MILES

2,200 FT

6-8 HOURS

Classic North Cascades hike that passes by seven beautiful lakes while also offering some stunning views of Bird Mountain along the way. This is a well-maintained trail with moderate elevation gain at the start but it gets easier as the hike goes on except for one little elevation bump near the end. Gentle inclines. Gentle declines. Pretty flat in-between. This is a pretty solid recommendation if you want to go out on a pretty decent hike but had not made plans to take another trail. It's worth the work and is pretty the whole way.

CURRENT CONDITIONS:

Pretty much ideal. No gear required.

CASCADE PASS - COTTONWOOD CAMP

[Out and Back]

MODERATE
MAINTAINED

17.6 MILES

4,500 FT

9-11 HOURS

Historic pass with incredible mountain panoramas that will take your breath away. The initial climb up to the pass may seem rather daunting - so many switchbacks - but then you will descend to and follow the Stehkein River until you reach Cottonwood Camp. Scrambling over fallen trees and bushwacking may be required on the later, less maintained sections of the trail.

CURRENT CONDITIONS:

Somewhat overgrown. Downed trees. Potential for icy conditions during the early morning. Microspikes and ice axe recommended.

[Out and Back]

EASY
MAINTAINED

4.6 MILES

525 FT

1-3 HOURS

I hate this trail. It's a do-nothing hike that shitty RV campers (no offense) pick out of a brochure in order to say that they hiked in the North Cascades. BLECH! GROSS! TERRIBLE! It's not even technical or challenging at any point. Fucking mountain bikers use this trail (no offense) and an old hat once told me he saw a four year-old toddle the entire thing in one go. The views are nice, I guess, and the parts where you actually touch the river can sometimes slippery. I absolutely fucking love hiking trails with rivers, rapids, waterfalls, pools, ponds, and lakes and I still just do not like this trail one bit. This is only here because Lilia told me we needed to add more "family friendly" trails. Well this is as "family friendly" as a walk through the woods could ever be. You might as well be walking around your local park.

CURRENT CONDITIONS:

Boring. Busy. Perfect night hike

LOST RIVER TRAIL

[Out and Back]

EASY
CAUTION

8 MILES

600 FT

4-6 HOURS

The Lost River Trail certainly has a fitting name. While there is a parking lot at the trailhead, I have never seen another car parked there in all my years hiking these mountains. It's a solitary and short trail with a lot of heart. Perfect for family hikers who want to spend the day by the water without anyone else bugging them.

Then you reach the fourth mile marker and Lost River plays its trump card, a 4,600 foot elevation gain in less than two miles with absolutely no tree cover. I am not going to lie and tell you that going the extra four miles to reach Pistol Pass are worth it - they most certainly are not - but going up the canyon for about 1/2 mile does give you a great, downstream view of the Lost River as it breaks towards Gardner Mountain. If you choose to hike the extra miles to Pistol Pass, however, have fun baking in the sun and tell me all about your sunburns when you get back.

CURRENT CONDITIONS:

Somewhat overgrown. Downed trees.

CASCADE PASS - SAHALE PEAK

[Out and Back]

HARD
ADVANCED

12.8 MILES

5,000 FT

9-11 HOURS

Challenging extension that splits off from Cascade Pass. Instead of heading down into the valley from the pass as you would for Cottonwood Camp, you'll instead be taking an old prospector's route that takes you up towards the Sahale Glacier and, eventually, Sahale Peak. Much of the trail is completely exposed and scrambling is a must-have skill in order to reach the base of the glacier. Choosing to continue past this point requires ice and glacier climbing experience. If you do not have these things, do not go out on the glacier. Making it to the top, however, rewards you with a beautiful view of Forbidden Peak, Eldorado Peak, and the volcanic Glacier Peak.

CURRENT CONDITIONS:

Snow and ice above 6,500 ft. Full alpine gear required. Glacier experience required. Don't be fucking stupid.
